# Title Patterns by Channel

Snapshot based on the current public `shipshitshow/vault` entries and their `view_count` values.

## Long-form winner patterns

### 1. Direct comparison or verdict

Examples:

- `GPT 5.4 vs Claude Opus 4.6: It's Not Even Close`

Why it works:

- Clear conflict.
- Immediate decision framing.
- Named entities with existing demand.

### 2. Product conflict or displacement

Examples:

- `Claude Code Channels Just Killed OpenClaw.`

Why it works:

- "X killed Y" is fast to parse.
- Signals stakes, winners, losers, and product relevance in one line.

### 3. Disaster or breakdown framing

Examples:

- `1 Million AI Agents Built a Social Network. It Was a Disaster`

Why it works:

- Big number plus aftermath.
- Promises story, failure, and spectacle.

### 4. Practical tutorial when the product pull is already strong

Examples:

- `How to Install OpenClaw Agent Swarm on Discord`

Why it works:

- Works when there is already strong demand for the named tool.
- Lower-drama, higher-intent traffic.

## Long-form weak or risky patterns

These are not banned, but they need stronger supporting demand or a better hook:

- abstract philosophical framing without a concrete enemy
- topic names that have not yet earned attention
- titles that sound like internal context instead of public conflict

Recent example with weak current count:

- `Claude Mythos: Anthropic Won't Let You Use It`

Use caution here:

- the topic may still be fresh
- low counts can be distorted by recency
- the skill should compare freshness before over-learning from it

## Shorts winner patterns

### 1. Fast verdicts

Examples:

- `GPT 5.4 vs Opus 4.6: The Verdict #shorts`

### 2. AGI / control / shocking capability

Examples:

- `OpenClaw Controls My Computer?! (AGI is Here!)`

### 3. Job loss / existential stakes

Examples:

- `Jack just fired 4,000 people. AI changed everything. #shorts`
- `Don't Get Replaced By AI #shorts`

### 4. Automation and output gain

Examples:

- `Automate. Scale AI, 10X Output #shorts`
- `AI Automates Bug Fixing Pipeline #shorts`

### 5. Controversy around powerful restricted systems

Examples:

- `Claude Mythos: Too Powerful to Withhold? #shorts`

## Reusable title guidance

Long-form:

- allow bigger claim lines
- reward conflict, comparison, collapse, or reversal
- keep named products in the line when they already carry demand

Shorts:

- shorten the setup
- front-load the hook
- make the payoff legible in under 2 seconds

Avoid:

- lifeless keyword strings
- generic "complete guide" language unless the archive clearly supports it
- vague thought-leadership phrasing that hides the actual event
