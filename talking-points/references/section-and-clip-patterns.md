# Section and Clip Patterns

## Intro pattern

A strong Ship Sh!t Show intro usually does four things quickly:

1. names the event or conflict
2. says why it matters
3. gives the first thesis
4. hints that the obvious conclusion is incomplete or wrong

Example pattern:

> X just happened. On the surface it looks like Y. That part is true. But the real story is Z.

## Section pattern

Each section should have one job:

- explain what happened
- compare two things
- show what the mainstream take misses
- prove the thesis with evidence
- show the practical implication

Do not let sections blur together.

## Fair-then-pivot pattern

This shows up a lot in the vault:

- agree with the strongest part of the opposing argument
- name the real problem clearly
- pivot to the deeper conclusion

Example pattern:

> They are right about the problem. They are wrong about the conclusion.

## Clip pattern

Good clips are not summaries. They are self-contained tension lines.

Use this shape:

```text
✂️ CLIP N: <hook line> (<duration>)
```

Strong clip types:

- direct conflict
- shocking stat
- simple verdict
- hard reversal
- practical takeaway

## Distribution pattern

When distribution notes are useful, split them by likely repackaging:

- one or more shorts
- one social thread angle
- one Reddit or builder-community angle
